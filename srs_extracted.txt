1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document defines the complete functional, non-functional, system, and compliance requirements for the Agentic AI Video Call–Based Onboarding System developed for Poonawalla Fincorp’s Loan Wizard platform. It serves as the authoritative reference for developers, architects, QA engineers, product managers, compliance officers, and all other stakeholders involved in the design, development, and deployment of the system.

1.2 Scope
The system, referred to as the Loan Wizard Onboarding Platform (LWOP), enables end-to-end digital loan origination through a live video call interface. The platform integrates Speech-to-Text (STT), Computer Vision, Large Language Models (LLM), bureau data, policy engines, and risk scoring to fully automate loan eligibility verification and personalized offer generation without requiring physical presence or paper documents.

The scope encompasses:
Customer entry via digital campaign links
Live video call–based data capture and identity validation
Speech-to-Text consent and information extraction
Facial analysis and age estimation via Computer Vision
Auto-fill of loan application from extracted data
Risk assessment using internal bureau data, ML models, and policy rules
LLM-based conversational intelligence and customer classification
Personalized loan offer generation
Central audit logging, compliance storage, and reporting

3. Functional Requirements
3.1 Customer Entry via Campaign Link
FR-1.1: Link Generation and Delivery
The system SHALL generate unique, tokenized, single-use URLs for each campaign target.
Links SHALL be deliverable via SMS, WhatsApp, email, and in-app notifications.
Each link SHALL carry a JWT token embedding: customer mobile number, campaign ID, expiry timestamp, and channel source.
Links SHALL expire after 72 hours or upon first session initiation, whichever is earlier.
The system SHALL validate the token on every access attempt and return a 401 error for expired or tampered tokens.

FR-1.2: Session Initialization
Upon link access, the system SHALL collect device capability information (camera, microphone availability).
The system SHALL prompt the user to grant camera and microphone permissions before initiating the session.
The system SHALL display a pre-session checklist: ID document readiness, lighting conditions, quiet environment.
Session initialization SHALL complete within 5 seconds on a 4G connection.

3.2 Video Call Management
FR-2.1: Real-Time Video Session
The system SHALL establish a WebRTC-based peer-to-peer (or SFU-routed) video call session.
Video SHALL be captured at minimum 720p resolution at 25 fps.
Audio SHALL be captured at minimum 16 kHz sampling rate, mono channel.
The system SHALL support session durations of up to 30 minutes.
The system SHALL display a real-time video quality indicator to the customer.
If connectivity drops below acceptable thresholds, the system SHALL attempt reconnection for up to 60 seconds before terminating the session.

FR-2.2: Session Recording
All video and audio streams SHALL be recorded in real time.
Recordings SHALL be encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256).
Recordings SHALL be tagged with: session ID, customer ID (tokenized), timestamp, and geo-location.
The system SHALL generate a SHA-256 hash of each recording file upon completion for tamper detection.

3.3 Speech-to-Text (STT) and Consent Capture
FR-3.1: Real-Time Transcription
The system SHALL stream audio to an STT service with latency under 2 seconds per utterance.
The STT service SHALL support Indian English, Hindi, Marathi, and Tamil at minimum.
Transcription confidence scores SHALL be captured and stored per segment.
Low-confidence segments (below 80%) SHALL be flagged for human review.

FR-3.3: Consent Audit Trail
The system SHALL timestamp and store the exact phrase used to provide consent.
Consent records SHALL be immutable post-capture.
The system SHALL generate a consent receipt (PDF) sent to the customer’s registered email/WhatsApp.

3.4 Computer Vision – Age Estimation
FR-4.1: Facial Detection and Analysis
The system SHALL detect a human face within the video frame within 3 seconds of session start.
If no face is detected, the system SHALL prompt the customer to reposition and retry up to 3 times.
Liveness detection SHALL be performed to prevent spoofing via photographs or screens.

FR-4.2: Age Estimation
The CV model SHALL estimate customer age with a target accuracy of ±3 years (Mean Absolute Error).
If estimated age is below 18 years, the session SHALL be immediately terminated and flagged.
If estimated age deviates from declared age by more than 10 years, the record SHALL be flagged for manual review.
Age estimation confidence score SHALL be stored and used as a fraud signal.

3.5 Geo-Location Capture and Validation
FR-5.1: Location Capture
The system SHALL request GPS location from the customer’s device upon session start.
If GPS is unavailable, the system SHALL fall back to IP-based geo-location (accuracy ±10 km).
Location SHALL be captured at start, middle (15 minutes), and end of session.

FR-5.2: Fraud Signal Generation
If customer location is outside India, the session SHALL be flagged and may be blocked per policy.
If location changes by more than 50 km between captures, the system SHALL flag as a fraud signal.
Location data SHALL be compared against the customer’s registered address for discrepancy scoring.

3.6 Auto-Fill and Application Form Generation
FR-6.1: Data Mapping
All fields extracted via STT and CV SHALL automatically populate the digital loan application form.
The auto-fill mapping SHALL cover a minimum of 85% of all required application fields.
Remaining fields SHALL be prompted interactively during the video call.

FR-6.2: Data Validation
Each auto-filled field SHALL pass format and range validation before submission.
Conflicting data (e.g., STT vs. CV vs. bureau mismatch) SHALL generate a discrepancy flag.
Customer SHALL be given an opportunity to confirm auto-filled data before submission.

3.7 Risk and Policy Evaluation Engine
FR-7.1: Bureau Data Integration
The system SHALL query internal credit bureau records using customer PAN or Aadhaar (masked) in real time.
Bureau data retrieval SHALL complete within 3 seconds with a 99.5% SLA.
The following bureau data SHALL be consumed: credit score, existing loan obligations, payment history (24 months), defaults and write-offs, inquiries in the last 6 months.

FR-7.2: Policy Rule Evaluation
The system SHALL evaluate a configurable rule engine against: minimum income thresholds, credit score bands, employment tenure, geographic eligibility, negative list (blacklist) check, loan amount limits by segment.
Policy rules SHALL be version-controlled and support hot-reload without system restart.
The policy engine SHALL return a pass/fail decision with a reason code within 1 second.

FR-7.3: ML Risk Scoring
The system SHALL invoke an ML propensity and risk scoring model with the following inputs: bureau score, income, employment type, loan purpose, age, location, session fraud signals.
The model SHALL return: credit risk band (Low/Medium/High/Very High), default probability score (0–1), propensity-to-pay score, recommended loan amount range.

3.8 LLM Intelligence Layer
FR-8.1: Conversational Context Understanding
The LLM SHALL process the complete STT transcript to understand intent, tone, and context.
The LLM SHALL normalize unstructured responses (e.g., “about 50 thousand” → 50000).
The LLM SHALL detect inconsistencies or evasive responses within the transcript.

FR-8.2: Customer Classification
The LLM SHALL output a structured JSON object containing: customer persona label, risk confidence indicator (0–1), key extracted entities, anomaly flags, and summary narrative.
LLM outputs SHALL be strictly advisory and SHALL NOT override deterministic policy or bureau decisions.
All LLM prompts and responses SHALL be logged for auditability.
The system SHALL use prompt versioning; any prompt change SHALL require a deployment pipeline review.

3.9 Loan Offer Generation
FR-9.1: Offer Calculation
The system SHALL generate up to 3 loan offer variants differing in: principal amount, interest rate, tenure, EMI.
Offers SHALL be calculated using risk band, policy limits, and customer-declared preferences.
Interest rates SHALL be fetched from the real-time rate card API with a maximum 5-second cache TTL.

FR-9.2: Offer Presentation
Offers SHALL be presented to the customer on-screen during or immediately after the video call.
Each offer SHALL display: eligible loan amount, annual interest rate (APR), tenure options, monthly EMI, total repayment amount, processing fee.
The customer SHALL be able to select and digitally accept an offer within the session.

3.10 Central Audit Repository
FR-10.1: Data Storage
The following records SHALL be stored for every completed session: encrypted video recording, STT transcript, consent record, geo-location timestamps, CV age estimation result and score, extracted application data, bureau query and response, policy evaluation result and reason codes, ML scores, LLM input/output, generated loan offers, customer’s selected offer (if any).

FR-10.2: Retention and Immutability
All session records SHALL be retained for a minimum of 5 years per RBI guidelines.
Audit records SHALL be immutable – no update or delete operations SHALL be permitted post-write.
Records SHALL support legal hold tagging to extend retention beyond standard policy.

FR-10.3: Access Control for Audit Data
Only authorized roles (Compliance Officer, Auditor, System Administrator) SHALL have read access to raw session recordings.
All access to audit records SHALL be logged with user ID, timestamp, and purpose.

5.2 Security Requirements
All PII SHALL be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3).
Authentication for internal users SHALL use MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication).
The system SHALL implement OWASP API Security Top 10 mitigations.
API rate limiting SHALL be enforced: maximum 100 requests/minute per IP; maximum 10 concurrent sessions per operator.
All authentication tokens SHALL have a maximum lifetime of 15 minutes with refresh token rotation.
Vulnerability assessments SHALL be conducted quarterly; penetration tests annually.
The system SHALL support IP allowlisting for internal service-to-service communication.
No customer PII SHALL appear in application logs. Log fields SHALL use tokenized/masked representations.

5.3 Reliability and Availability
The system SHALL have a target availability of 99.9% (approximately 8.7 hours downtime per year).
Scheduled maintenance SHALL occur during off-peak hours (2:00 AM – 5:00 AM IST) with advance notification.
The system SHALL implement automated health checks and self-healing for critical services.
All critical services SHALL have redundant instances deployed across at least 2 availability zones.
Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 15 minutes; Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 5 minutes.

5.4 Scalability
The system SHALL support horizontal scaling of all microservices independently.
Auto-scaling SHALL trigger when CPU utilization exceeds 70% for 2 consecutive minutes.
The video processing tier SHALL scale from 100 to 2,000 concurrent sessions within 5 minutes.
The database tier SHALL support read replicas for horizontal scaling of read operations.

5.5 Usability
The customer-facing UI SHALL achieve a System Usability Scale (SUS) score of at least 75.
The onboarding flow SHALL be completable in under 15 minutes for a first-time user.
UI text SHALL be available in English, Hindi, and Marathi at minimum.
The UI SHALL comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards.
Error messages SHALL be human-readable with actionable guidance.

5.6 Maintainability
All microservices SHALL expose standardized health-check and readiness endpoints.
Code coverage for unit tests SHALL be maintained at 80% minimum.
The system SHALL support blue-green deployments with zero-downtime rollouts.
All configuration (policy rules, thresholds, prompts) SHALL be externalized from the codebase.

6. System Architecture
6.1 Architecture Overview
The LWOP follows a cloud-native microservices architecture organized into five logical tiers: Customer Interaction Layer, Orchestration Layer, AI/ML Processing Layer, Data and Storage Layer, and Admin/Compliance Layer. All tiers communicate through an API Gateway with mTLS authentication between services.

6.3 Data Flow
The high-level data flow follows these sequential stages during a customer session:
Customer accesses campaign link → Campaign Service validates token → Session context created.
Video Session Service establishes WebRTC connection → audio/video streams begin recording.
Audio stream routed simultaneously to STT Processing Service → real-time transcription begins.
Video frames sampled every 5 seconds → sent to CV Age Estimation Service.
Geo-Location Service captures GPS/IP coordinates at session start.
STT transcript passed to Data Extraction Service → NLP entity extraction → form auto-fill.
Extracted data + bureau query sent to Policy Engine Service → eligibility decision.
Eligible applications: ML Scoring Service invoked → risk band assigned.
Full transcript + context sent to LLM Intelligence Service → classification JSON returned.
Offer Engine Service calculates personalized offers → presented to customer.
All session artifacts stored in Audit Service → Notification Service sends consent receipt.

8. Compliance and Regulatory Requirements
8.1 KYC Compliance (RBI)
The system SHALL comply with RBI Master Direction on KYC, 2016 and all subsequent amendments.
Video KYC SHALL capture: customer face, government-issued ID document on camera, verbal declaration of name and date of birth.
Video KYC sessions SHALL be conducted only with a live agent or AI proctoring as specified by RBI.
Video recordings SHALL be retained for a minimum of 5 years.

8.2 Data Protection (DPDPA 2023)
The system SHALL obtain explicit, informed, and revocable digital consent before collecting any personal data.
Customers SHALL have the right to access, correct, and request erasure of their data (subject to regulatory retention requirements).
A Data Processing Agreement SHALL be in place for all third-party service providers.
Data breach notification SHALL occur within 72 hours of discovery.

8.3 Information Security
The system SHALL comply with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 standards.
Annual third-party security audits SHALL be conducted.
Penetration testing SHALL be conducted before major releases and annually.

8.4 Audit and Traceability
Every decision (eligibility, offer, rejection) SHALL be traceable to its source data and rule set.
The system SHALL generate an explanation for every AI-assisted decision in plain language.
Audit logs SHALL capture: who accessed what data, when, and from where.
